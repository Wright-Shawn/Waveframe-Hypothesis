Core Equations and Formulas of the Waveframe Hypothesis

1. Klein–Gordon Equation for Scalar Field Evolution
Describes the time evolution of the scalar field φ(t):

ϕ̈ + 3Hϕ̇ + (Λ⁴ / f) * sin(ϕ / f) = 0

- ϕ: Scalar field
- H: Hubble parameter
- Λ: Energy scale of the potential
- f: Field decay constant

Reference: Turner, M.S. (1983). Coherent scalar-field oscillations in an expanding universe. Phys. Rev. D, 28(6), 1243.

2. Scalar Field Potential (Axion-like Form)
Defines the potential energy of the scalar field:

V(ϕ) = Λ⁴ [1 - cos(ϕ / f)]

This is a periodic potential, characteristic of axion-like or pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson fields.

Reference: Freese, K., Frieman, J.A., & Olinto, A.V. (1990). Natural inflation with pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons. Phys. Rev. Lett., 65, 3233.

3. Friedmann Equation (Flat Universe)
Relates the Hubble parameter to the energy content:

H² = (1 / 3Mₚ²) [½ϕ̇² + V(ϕ) + ρ_m + ρ_r]

- Mₚ: Reduced Planck mass
- ρ_m: Matter energy density
- ρ_r: Radiation energy density

4. Redshift–Scale Factor Relation

1 + z = 1 / a(t)

Where:
- z: Cosmological redshift
- a(t): Scale factor (normalized so a = 1 today)

5. Equation of State Parameter (Dynamic Dark Energy)

w(ϕ) = p_ϕ / ρ_ϕ

Where:
p_ϕ = ½ϕ̇² - V(ϕ)
ρ_ϕ = ½ϕ̇² + V(ϕ)

This characterizes the "thawing" or "freezing" nature of dark energy evolution.

Reference: Caldwell, R.R., & Linder, E.V. (2005). The limits of quintessence. Phys. Rev. Lett., 95, 141301.

6. Luminosity Distance

d_L(z) = (1 + z) * ∫₀ᶻ (dz' / H(z'))

This is used to compare the model's prediction to SN Ia data like Pantheon+.

7. Distance Modulus

μ(z) = 5 * log₁₀[d_L(z) / (10 pc)]

Used for comparison with supernova data.
